The text provides an overview of leveraged lending, including its characteristics, participants, and collateralized loan obligations (CLOs). It discusses the migration of activity from banks to nonbanks and the implications for systemic risk and regulation. The text also explains loan syndication, covenants in leveraged loan agreements, and the increase in covenant-lite loans. It examines the size and growth of the leveraged lending market and the regulation and potential risks associated with it. The risks to investors and overall financial stability presented by leveraged loans and CLOs are explored. The text addresses the regulatory fragmentation and different approaches to oversight for banks, nonbank investment funds, and insurance companies. The hearing held by the House Financial Services Committee on leveraged lending is mentioned, along with two draft bills aimed at addressing the potential risks.
